### Indicator 4.2: Patent enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies, provisional and other measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law of Turkmenistan No. 629-V on the Legal Protection of Inventions (Türkmenistanyň kanuny № 629-V Oýlap tapyşlaryň hukuk goragy hakynda)
- **Evidence/Impact:** Turkmenistan has implemented civil and administrative procedures and remedies, as well as provisional measures for copyright and related rights protection.

Article 39. Liability for Unlawful Use of an Invention: Any person who uses a patented invention in violation of this Law is considered to have infringed the exclusive rights of the patent holder and is subject to liability under Turkmenistan’s legislation. A person unlawfully using a patented invention, at the request of the patent holder or by court order, must:
- Recognize the rights of the patent holder;
- Cease actions that infringe on the patent holder’s rights;
- Compensate for damages, including lost profits, or pay compensation, the amount of which is determined by the court or by mutual agreement;
- Compensate for moral damages;
- Publish a court decision in order to restore the business reputation of the patent holder.
- The court has the right to order the confiscation of counterfeit goods.

A lawsuit for patent infringement may be filed within three years from the date the patent holder became aware or should have become aware of the infringement.
- The patent holder may file the lawsuit.
- The holder of an exclusive license may also file a lawsuit for patent infringement unless otherwise specified in the licensing agreement.
- The holder of a non-exclusive license may file a lawsuit only if explicitly permitted in the licensing agreement.
- **Timeframe:** Since November 2017

### Indicator 4.5: Online copyright enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies; and provisional measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law of Turkmenistan About Copyright and the Related Rights No. 257-IV (Türkmenistanyň kanuny Awtorlyk hukugy we gatyşyk hukuklar hakynda № 257-IV)
- **Evidence/Impact:** Turkmenistan has implemented civil and administrative procedures and remedies, as well as provisional measures for copyright and related rights protection.

Article 43. Copyright Infringement and Counterfeit Copies: Counterfeit copies are works or related rights objects produced or distributed in violation of copyright laws. This includes imports into Turkmenistan without the rights holder’s consent from countries where such works lack protection. Violations result in civil, criminal, and administrative liability.

Article 44. Methods of Copyright Protection: Rights holders can demand recognition of their rights, cessation of infringements, restoration of prior conditions, compensation for damages or lost profits, and other legal remedies. Courts may confiscate counterfeit copies, materials, and equipment used for their production, with options for destruction or transfer to the rights holder. Good-faith third-party acquisitions are exempt from confiscation.

Article 45. Technical Protection Measures: Technical protection measures control access to works and prevent unauthorized actions. Circumvention, production, or distribution of technologies that disable such protections is prohibited. Rights holders can claim damages or compensation for violations.
