### Indicator 4.5: Online copyright enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies; and provisional measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Copyright Act (Act No. 34 of 2023) 2023
- **Evidence/Impact:** Chapter 16 (Civil remedies) sections 77 to 83 addresses civil remedies for copyright infringement. 

Chapter 17 (Criminal Offenses and Punishments) sections 84 to 115 list all the criminal penalties and legal recourse to be taken to deal with copyright infringement. It lists task forces, investigation, trial in court, power of search and seizure and different penalties for a variety of offences. Specifically provision 100 deals with "Penalties for infringement of information technology-based digital works". Infringement of this work can result in inprisonment (3 months-4 years) and fine (1000 or 400000 rupees) depending on whether infringement was for profit or not. 

Additionally section 108 concerns power of search and seizure. A police officer or authorized officer, with reasonable grounds, can search and seize infringing copies, materials, or instruments used for infringement without a warrant. These items must be presented to a court. Within 15 days of seizure, anyone with an interest in the seized items can apply to the court for their return.
