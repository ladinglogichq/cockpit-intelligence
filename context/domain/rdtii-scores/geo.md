### Indicator 4.1: Patent application issues
- **Score:** 1
- **Source Legislation:** Patent Law of Georgia No.1791 1999
- **Evidence/Impact:** Law is in force since 1999. The Law on Patents in Georgia provides equal rights for both local and foreign applicants. There is no formal differential treatment in terms of substantive patent law, and the criteria for patentability (novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability) are identical for all applicants, regardless of their nationality. 

Specifically, Article 22.1 (Appointment of patent attorney) provides that the applicant or patent owner who does not have a permanent place of residence or a registered legal address in Georgia, is obliged to appoint a patent attorney of Georgia as a presentative in the patent. If the applicant or the patent owner does not fulfill the requirements specified, the patentee makes a decision to terminate the proceedings on the application.

Article 24 (application) prescribes that the application for obtaining a patent must include a) an application for obtaining a patent; b) the description of the invention; c) invention formula; d) drawings and other documents, if they are necessary to explain the essence of the invention; and e) the abstract of the invention. In case of appointing a representative of the applicant, a document confirming the authority of the representative must be attached to the application immediately after its submission or within 2 months from the date of its submission.

However, the application must be submitted in the state language of Georgia, other application materials - in any language. In case of submission of application materials in a foreign language, the applicant must submit their Georgian translation within 2 months from the date of submission of the application. If it is not submitted, the proceedings on the application will be terminated.
- **Timeframe:** Since May 1999, last amended in May 2023
- **References:** https://matsne.gov.ge/ka/document/view/11470?publication=10
