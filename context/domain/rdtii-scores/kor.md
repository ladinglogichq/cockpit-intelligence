### Indicator 1.4: Imposing trade defence measures (anti-dumping, countervailing duties and safeguards) on ICT-related products imported by other Asia-Pacific economies
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Customs Act (관세법)
- **Evidence/Impact:** There are no anti-dumping, countervailing duty or safeguard measures against ICT products.

Republic of Korea's Customs Act provides the legal framework for implementing trade defense measures—such as anti-dumping duties, countervailing duties, and safeguards—to protect domestic industries from unfair trade practices. These provisions apply to all imported goods, including Information and Communication Technology (ICT) products. These provisions empower the Republic of Korea Trade Commission (KTC) to conduct investigations and recommend appropriate measures to the Ministry of Economy and Finance, which enforces them.

Many of Republic of Korea's trade defense actions, as seen in WTO reports and Republic of Korea Trade Commission (KTC) announcements, target steel, raw materials, and chemicals, but there is no ICT-specific measures, such as duties on semiconductors, chips, or telecommunication devices.
- **Timeframe:** Since 1949, last amended in December 2023

### Indicator 2.3: Limitations in procurement bidding
- **Score:** 0.00
- **Source Legislation:** Act on Contracts to Which the State is a Party (국가를 당사자로 하는 계약에 관한 법률) ;

Enforcement Decree of the Act on Contracts to Which the State is a Party (국가를 당사자로 하는 계약에 관한 법 시행령)
- **Evidence/Impact:** Since its enactment in 1995, the Act on Contracts to which the State is a Party, under Article 5, has prohibited "any special term or condition, discriminative against nationals of contracting states to the Agreement on Government Procurement and goods produced or services provided by such States and in favor of nationals of the Republic of Republic of Korea and goods produced or services provided from the Republic of Republic of Korea, to international tendering procedures." 

The Act on Contracts to Which a Local Government is a Party, under Article 6, has applied the same principle since 2005. These provisions apply horizontally across sectors. Per Article 12, the contracting agency may not restrict participation of bidders who have gone through a competitive bidding process. 

However, the Enforcement Decree of the Act on Contracts to Which the State is a Party, which was promulgated in 1995, provides some situations where a competitive bidding process is limited or does not apply, creating lack of transparency. For example, in light of factors such as the purpose, characteristics, and scale of the procurement contract, the government may restrict the participation of potential bidders (Article 21), select certain bidders to engage in further bidding (Article 23), or execute an at-will contract (Article 26). In this situation, at least five bidders must be chosen, and a minimum of two bidders from the selected group must agree to participate in the bidding process. If there are fewer than five available bidders, all eligible bidders must be included to ensure full participation in the bidding. Nevertheless, this does not necessarily amount to discrimination against foreign firms.

- **Evidence/Impact:** According to Article 8 (Reporting Classification) of the Notice of Import Customs Clearance for Express Goods, issued by the Korea Customs Service (E-commerce Customs Clearance Division), customs clearance procedures for special delivery items reflect a tiered system based on the value of the goods. 

Items intended for personal use or as duty-free commercial samples received by domestic residents and valued at USD 150 or less (or USD 200 or less for items subject to special customs clearance under the agreement with the United States) are referred to as “list-cleared express delivery items.” For these items, a formal import declaration under Article 241, Paragraph 1 of the Act may be omitted. Items exceeding USD 2,000 in value, termed “general import declaration express goods,” must undergo the standard import declaration process under Article 241, Paragraph 1 of the Act.
