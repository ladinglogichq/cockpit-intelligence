### Indicator 2.3: Limitations in procurement bidding
- **Score:** 1
- **Source Legislation:** Law on the Procurement of Goods, Works, and Services by State and Local Property Assets
- **Evidence/Impact:** Under Article 8 of the Law on the Procurement of Goods, Works, and Services by State and Local Property Assets, preference is given to domestic goods meeting quality standards. Article 8.1 prioritizes goods from domestic production, with a list developed by the central state administrative organization and approved by the Government (Article 8.2). Additional preferences include goods from recycled materials, those using local resources, innovative products, and those produced by businesses employing disabled workers (Article 8.4). 

Moreover, Article 8.5 gives preference to participants using domestic goods in their work. Discounts are provided on tender prices: 10% for domestic goods, or 15% if from small or medium-sized enterprises (Article 8.6). For work tenders, reductions are given for local operations and Mongolian-owned entities (Article 8.7). However, general contracts do not receive these preferences (Article 8.8), and tender documents must specify the required information for priority calculations (Article 8.9).

Note that the term "general contract method" means purchasing goods and services from the online store by entering into a general contract with one or more participants that sets the maximum unit price and other conditions for the goods and services that the customer frequently purchases or regularly needs for a certain period of time (Article 4.1.8).

According to Article 15.2, an intellectual property agent may represent a patent application. ### Indicator 4.2: Patent enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies, provisional and other measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law of Mongolia on Patent
- **Evidence/Impact:** The Patent Law of Mongolia addresses civil and administrative procedures, remedies, and provisional measures. Chapter 7 (Articles 46 to 52) focuses on the protection of patent rights and patent owners' rights. According to Articles 46.2.3 and 46.2.4, patent owners have the right to protect their inventions, utility models, and product designs from unauthorized use and to file lawsuits in court to enforce their rights. Article 55 (Liability for Violators) states that any person or legal entity violating this law shall be subject to the provisions of the Criminal Code or held liable as prescribed by law.

Additionally, provisional measures are addressed under Article 53. Chapter 8 outlines the role of the intellectual property authority and the dispute resolution council. Specifically, state inspectors have the power to stop violations (Article 53.1.3), seize infringing items (Article 53.1.3), prohibit sales (Article 53.1.4), and destroy infringing products (Article 53.1.4). These actions serve as provisional measures to prevent further harm to the rightsholder while a legal determination is pending.
- **Timeframe:** Since 1983, last amended May 2021

### Indicator 4.5: Online copyright enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies; and provisional measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law of Mongolia on Copyrights
- **Evidence/Impact:** The Copyright Law Chapter 7 (Articles 17 to 25) prescribes copyright protection to particular works, including computer program (Article 22) and database (Article 23). Regarding infringement and liability, Article 56 concerns infringement of copyright and related rights. The infringement also covers the placement, making internet (digital) linking or development of works in the social network without authorization of right holder (Article 56.1.5). Moreover, Article 57 addresses the consequences of copyright infringement. Individuals who violate copyright law face penalties as outlined in the Criminal Code or the Law on Infringement. Compensation for damages resulting from the infringement of exclusive rights is governed by the Civil Code. Additionally, devices and technology used for unauthorized reproduction of copyrighted works will be confiscated and become state property.

However, the exploitation of work or objects without an authorization legitimate software products remain rare in Mongolia, with the General Authority for Intellectual Property and State Registration (GAIPSR) estimating in early 2017 that at least 85 percent of the domestic market uses pirated software. GAIPSR enforces the law where it can but the scale of the problem dwarfs its capacity to deal with it. Second, pirated optical media are also readily available and subject to spotty anti-piracy enforcement. The growth of online downloads of pirated digital media by individuals, local Mongolian TV stations using pirated videos, radio broadcasters playing pirated music, and cellular service providers offering pirated ringtones has eclipsed local production and imports of fake CDs, videos, and DVDs. GAIPSR acknowledges that most local public and privately held TV stations, some 184 at latest count, regularly broadcast pirated materials; however, GAIPSR hesitates to move on these broadcasters, most of which are connected to major government or political figures. GAIPSR rarely initiates action on its own without prompts from rights holders.
- **Timeframe:** Since January 2006, last amended in May 2021
