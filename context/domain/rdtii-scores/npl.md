- **Evidence/Impact:** Directives on the Operation of Social Networking (2023) is issued under the Electronic Transactions Act 2008.

The provisions under Articles 5, 6, and 7 of the Directives establish a local presence requirement for social network platforms operating in Nepal. Article 6 (Provision of Point of Contact to be made) explicitly requires all social network platform operators to arrange a point of contact within Nepal for the purpose of addressing user grievances. In cases where a platform does not have an office in Nepal at the time the Directives come into force, the operator must either establish an office or designate a contact person within three months.

Additionally, Article 5 (Classification of Social Network Platform and Grievance Handling) requires platforms with more than 100,000 users—defined as “big social network platforms”—to appoint a residential grievance handling officer and a compliance officer responsible for monitoring adherence to self-regulation obligations. These officers must be available at the local point of contact. Article 7 (Responsibilities of Point of Contact) prescribes responsibilities of point of contact, such as identify contents disseminated in social networks that are contrary to these Directives.
