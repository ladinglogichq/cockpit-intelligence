### Indicator 4.01: Patent application issues
- **Score:** 1
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Patents 1999
- **Evidence/Impact:** Since 1999, the law regulates patent registration and protection, allowing both local and foreign inventors to secure patents. There are no major restrictions on foreign companies. Article 6 prescribes Rights of foreigners that in accordance with international agreements or on the principle of reciprocity, foreign individuals and organizations enjoy the rights provided for by this law and bear responsibility on an equal footing with the citizens and organizations of the Republic of Armenia.

However, Article 40 prescribes the requirements submitted to the application. The application shall include documents, for example, an application for obtaining a license; description of the invention; the definition of the claim of the invention; drawings and other materials; and a brief description of the invention. Moreover, Article 80 (Representation of foreign persons) prescribes that foreign natural persons and organizations that do not have a permanent place of residence or a real and operating industrial or commercial establishment in the territory of the Republic of Armenia (hereinafter referred to as "foreign persons"), conduct matters related to the acquisition and protection of their rights in the state authorized body through a representative.

### Indicator 4.2: Patent enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies, provisional and other measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Patents 1999
- **Evidence/Impact:** Since 1999, the law regulates patent registration and protection, allowing both local and foreign inventors to secure patents. There are no major restrictions on foreign companies.

Article 17 addresses the unauthorized use of patented industrial property, defining it as an infringement. When infringement occurs, the patent owner or, if permitted by contract, an exclusive or non-exclusive licensee, can demand the cessation of the infringing activity. The infringer is also required to compensate the patent owner for any resulting losses, following Armenian legislation.

Article 25 allows any person to lodge an appeal against the grant of a patent during the universal disclosure period of the patent application. Appeals must be reasoned and submitted within a four-month timeframe after the official publication of the patent application. Upon receiving an appeal, the Office sends a copy to the applicant and requests any relevant comments. The procedural aspects of filing, lodging, and reviewing appeals are defined by the Office.

Article 36 addresses the "Responsibility for Infringement of the Rights of the Creators of Inventions, Utility Models, and Industrial Designs." This article describes the penalties and responsibilities for those who unlawfully disclose or usurp inventors' rights before official publication, outlining provisional measures that protect the confidentiality and rights associated with the patent application process.

These articles collectively establish a framework for the enforcement of patent rights in Armenia, providing patent holders with legal avenues to protect their inventions and seek remedies against infringement.

### Indicator 5.1: Absence of passive infrastructure sharing
- **Score:** 0.5
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Electronic Communications 2005
- **Evidence/Impact:** Armenia's Law on Electronic Communications, adopted in 2005, Article 39 (Co-location) is a part of the Chapter 6 (Interconnection) requires that an operator shall be obliged to ensure, upon reasonable rates and conditions, the co-location by any other operator or service provider of such equipment that are needed for interconnection with, or access to its public electronic communications network within the premises of the operator. The operator may provide virtual co-location if it proves that their co-location is not practically feasible for technical reasons or due to space limitations. This includes providing physical space, power, lighting, and connections. If physical co-location isn’t feasible, virtual co-location must be offered. Operators are also required to share essential infrastructure, facilities, and information needed by other providers to deliver their services, promoting efficient network sharing and competition.

However, the Article 39 (Co-location) is a part of the Chapter 6 (Interconnection) of the Law on Electronic Communications, and therefore, it relates to passive infrastructure sharing only in the context of interconnection of public electronic communication networks of operators, and not in general.

Moreover, it promotes fair competition and the efficient use of resources, which indirectly supports infrastructure sharing practices. Key articles, including Article 1 and Article 4, outline objectives to foster open competition and manage resources effectively.

In practice, passive infrastructure sharing does occur within Armenia's telecommunications market, as operators, like Viva-MTS, sometimes collaborate to share infrastructure to cut costs and improve network coverage.

### Indicator 5.5: Licensing requirements in telecom sector for operators
- **Score:** 0

- **Evidence/Impact:** In Armenia, telecom operators with Significant Market Power (SMP) are subject to certain regulatory requirements, but the licensing regime for such operators is not as strict or specific as in some other jurisdictions. The legal framework does not mandate additional licensing requirements explicitly for SMP operators, but it does provide mechanisms for special regulation and oversight to prevent anti-competitive behavior. The Law on Electronic Communications 2005 is the main legal framework governing telecommunications in Armenia. It grants authority to the Public Services Regulatory Commission (PSRC) to regulate telecom operators and ensure fair competition.

Section 2 regulates licenses. No person shall be entitled to hold and operate a public electronic communications network in the Republic of Armenia unless that person holds a network licence granted under this Section and under the complex licensing procedures as provided for by the Law of the Republic of Armenia “On licensing” (Article 15). Article 16 prescribes procedure for issuing a network license. Aplicants meet certain technical and financial qualifications, aligning the process with national objectives around electronic communications. Moreover, Article 22 of the law specifies that telecom operators, including those with significant market power, require licenses to operate. However, the law does not impose special licensing requirements for operators based solely on their SMP status. Instead, the PSRC may impose additional regulatory measures on SMP operators to prevent anti-competitive behavior.
