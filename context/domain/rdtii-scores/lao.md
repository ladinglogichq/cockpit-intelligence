### Indicator 3.4: Screening of investment and acquisitions
Exception: Anti-trust measures related to M&A are not considered a restriction, unless discriminatory.
- **Score:** 0.25
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Investment Promotion Act No.14/NA 2010
- **Evidence/Impact:** According to the Law on Investment Promotion Act Article 41 (Amended) (Concession investments),  concession investment is an investment for which an investor is authorized by the Government in accordance with relevant laws and regulations to develop and operate a business notably concession of land, development of special economic zones, zones for industrial processing for export, mining, electric energy development, aviation and telecommunication. Therefore, foreign investors cannot own land but can only lease it, which is a national treatment. Additionally, ISIC 6110, 6120 and 6130 are related to telecomminucation services. These sectors have to receive an investment license prior, thus a screening requirement for this sector.

Article 2 and Article 3 of the Decree describe the screening mechanisms for investments in Lao PDR. Investments in sectors listed as controlled, which impact national security, social stability, and the environment, require screening and approval by relevant agencies, facilitated by a one-stop service unit that collaborates with the committee for promotion and management of investment. For concession investments, such as land concessions and the development of special economic zones, the approval process is extended, with screening taking up to 65 working days. These mechanisms aim to ensure that investments align with national priorities, excluding those that threaten stability, security, or the environment. While the government promotes investment across various sectors, businesses that could negatively impact national stability or violate other regulations are excluded from this promotion.

The Telecommunications Law does not explicitly ban or impose direct foreign equity limits for telecommunications businesses (nor in the FDI as discussed in the FDI Pillar). Article 4 of the Telecommunications Law encourages domestic and foreign individuals or entities to invest in the telecommunications sector, indicating that foreign investments are allowed under the licensing system guidelines. Foreign entities must adhere to investment and enterprise operations laws, including the requirement to apply for a telecommunications business license.

### Indicator 4.8: Mandatory disclosure of trade secrets, such as source code and algorithms
- **Score:** 0

The proprietor also has the right to take legal action and claim compensation for damages resulting from infringements, as well as to prevent individuals, legal entities, or organizations from misappropriating the trade secret. Additionally, the proprietor may disclose, withdraw, utilize, or transfer the trade secret to others, setting conditions for maintaining confidentiality. Finally, the proprietor can enforce confidentiality obligations on individuals lawfully in control of the trade secret, with such obligations continuing beyond the termination of employment or contractual relationships, as long as the information remains confidential.

Note that the exceptions are the legitimate means by which third parties may lawfully obtain or use trade secret information without it being considered a violation of trade secret rights. These exceptions are not about mandatory disclosure of trade secrets.
