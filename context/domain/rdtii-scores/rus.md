- **Evidence/Impact:** Article 14 Providing the national regime for procurement 

14.1 When carrying out procurements, a national treatment regime is provided, ensuring that goods originating from a foreign state or a group of foreign states, as well as works and services respectively performed or provided by a foreign citizen or foreign legal entity, are granted equal conditions with goods of Russian origin, works, and services respectively performed or provided by a Russian citizen or Russian legal entity, except in cases where the Government of the Russian Federation adopts measures provided for in paragraph 1 of part 2 of this article.

c) Preferential treatment for goods of Russian origin (including those supplied as part of purchased works or services), as well as works and services respectively performed or provided by Russian entities.

14.4 If the Government of the Russian Federation establishes a ban on procurement as specified in subparagraph "a" of paragraph 1 of part 2 of this article:

a) Any bid for procurement that includes an offer to supply such a good originating from a foreign state shall be rejected in accordance with this Federal Law;

b) A contract for the supply of such a good originating from a foreign state cannot be concluded with a sole supplier;

c) During contract execution, the replacement of the good with one originating from a foreign state for which such a ban has been established is not permitted.

1. Definition of a Foreign Entity Operating on the Internet in Russia
A foreign entity operating on the Internet in the Russian Federation refers to any foreign legal entity engaged in any legal activity in Russia, under the condition that the entity:

- **Evidence/Impact:** Consumer rights in Russia are protected under the Consumer Rights Protection Law No. 2300-1-FZ, as well as relevant administrative, civil, and criminal procedure legislation. 

Even if foreign law governs the relationship—either through party choice or conflict-of-law rules—Russian consumer protection laws will still apply if the online business targets consumers in Russia.

As a result, Russian courts will assert jurisdiction over disputes involving electronic contracts whenever a foreign entity offers goods or services to Russian consumers online. For instance, Russian courts may assume jurisdiction if the defendant conducts business in Russia, if contractual performance is carried out in Russia, if property damage or a related event occurs in Russia, or in other circumstances demonstrating a close connection to the country.
