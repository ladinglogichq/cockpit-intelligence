### Indicator 2.1: Foreign exclusions from public procurement related to ICT goods and digital services
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Public Procurement No. 988-VIQ
- **Evidence/Impact:** In Azerbaijan, “Law on Public Procurement" no 988 governs public procurement. The Law was adopted in July 14, 2023 and came into effect on January 1, 2024. 

According to Article 5.1 of the Law, any resident and non-resident supplier may participate in procurements held in the Republic of Azerbaijan, except for cases provided for by the international obligations of the Republic of Azerbaijan, or where restrictions are imposed on the participation of certain foreign suppliers or foreign suppliers from certain countries (including countries with which the Republic of Azerbaijan does not have diplomatic relations) for reasons of state security. ### Indicator 2.3: Limitations in procurement bidding
- **Score:** 1
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Public Procurement No. 988-VIQ
- **Evidence/Impact:** In Azerbaijan, “Law on Public Procurement" no 988 governs public procurement. The Law was adopted in July 14, 2023 and came into effect on January 1, 2024. The procurement stages and procedures are identified under Chapter 3 of the Law. Specifically, Article 26 provides that an iinivation to tender shall be published on the portal at least before the date of opening of tenders within the provided periods specified in the Law. 

The Law prioritizes transparency and value for money (Article 33). In most procurement methods, including open, closed, and two-stage tenders, the winning proposal is selected based on either the lowest price or the most economically advantageous offer that meets the requirements (Article 33.8). 

However, Article 33.13 introduces price preference. When evaluating proposals, the procurement commission applies a procurement discount of up to 20 percent in favor of a proposal for a procurement subject of local origin. The applied procurement discount is recorded in the final protocol. The procurement discount also applies to goods carried out in the territory of the Republic of Azerbaijan at any stage of the production process determined by the body (institution) designated by the relevant executive authority in order to stimulate investments in the production of non-oil products.
Note that under Article 1 Basic concepts, 1.1.24. procurement discount – a preferential adjustment applied to the prices offered for the same imported procurement item in favor of the offers submitted for the procurement item of local origin, in accordance with the procedure  when evaluating the offers. 

Moreover, regarding language, procurement documents must be in Azerbaijani. Translations into foreign languages are allowed, but the Azerbaijani version prevails in case of discrepancies (Article 12). Non-resident suppliers may submit documents in foreign languages if permitted by the tender conditions. While regarding currency, local suppliers must submit prices in Azerbaijani manat. Non-resident suppliers can use a specified foreign currency, but conversion will be based on the Central Bank’s exchange rate on the last submission day (Article 13).
- **Timeframe:** Since January 2024
- **References:** https://e-qanun.az/framework/54966

### Indicator 3.1: Foreign equity limits in sectors relevant to digital trade 
Exception: Not cover foreign equity cap in telecom sector (covered under Pillar 5) and e-commerce sector (covered under Pillar 12)
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law on Investment Activity No.551-VIQ
- **Evidence/Impact:** In Azerbaijan, foreign investment in sectors relevant to digital trade is governed by the Law on Investment Activity, which establishes the legal framework for both local and foreign investments. The Law entered into force in June 22, 2022. Upon entry into force of this Law, the Law on Protection of Foreign Investment repealed, which was in force since Jan 15, 1992.

Article 5.1 of the of the Law states that the main purpose of state policy in the field of investment activity is to ensure sustainable economic development through local and foreign investments, develop competitive production and service sectors, and introduce new technologies and innovations in the economy. Article 5.2.3 of the lists creating equal opportunities for investors in the implementation of investment activities among the main directions of state policy in the field of investment activity. 

Moreover, Article 12 addresses treatment of investors. The government guarantees to protect investors and their rights from discrimination, threats, or unfair treatment. Foreign investors must be treated equally to domestic investors in similar situations, and receive the same treatment as other foreign investors. 

There is no formal limitation on foreign investment in SOEs; however, some are structured as Closed Joint-Stock Companies (CJSCs). CJSCs are not publicly traded, and shareholders may only sell their shares with the consent of other shareholders. Thus, while technically more than 50% of shares can be sold, such transactions require shareholder approval.

- **Score:** 1

- **Evidence/Impact:** According to the Patent Law, Article 27 concerns filing of a patent application. Legal entities or individuals who want a patent for an invention, utility model, or industrial design must submit an application to the authority designated by the relevant executive body.
