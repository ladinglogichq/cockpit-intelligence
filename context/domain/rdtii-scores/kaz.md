### Indicator 3.4: Screening of investment and acquisitions
Exception: Anti-trust measures related to M&A are not considered a restriction, unless discriminatory.
- **Score:** 0.25
- **Source Legislation:** Decree of the Supreme Council No.268-ХIII on the Civil Code, 1994 (Қазақстан Республикасының Кодексі 1994 жылғы 27 желтоқсандағы № 268-ХIII);

Law No. 413-IV on State property, 2011 (Қазақстан Республикасының 2011 жылғы 1 наурыздағы N 413-IV Заңы)
- **Evidence/Impact:** Article 193-1 of Kazakhstan's Civil Code defines "strategic facilities" as assets vital to the nation's socio-economic stability and national security. These include critical infrastructure such as main railway networks, pipelines, national electrical grids, communication lines, and facilities related to television, radio broadcasting, and nuclear energy. The article stipulates that any encumbrance or alienation (transfer of ownership) of these strategic facilities requires prior approval from the Government of Kazakhstan, as outlined in the country's state property legislation. Additionally, if a private entity intends to sell a strategic facility, the Republic of Kazakhstan holds a preemptive right to purchase it under terms specified by state property laws. Transactions conducted without adhering to these requirements are deemed null and void.

According to Article 188 of Kazakhstan's Law on State Property, to proceed with any transfer or encumbrance of strategic facilities, the owner or holder of rights must submit a formal application to the appropriate state authority. This application requires an extensive array of documentation, including proof of ownership, a certified valuation of the facility, and crucially, comprehensive details on the intended acquirer, including final beneficiaries. 

Decisions to approve or deny applications for alienation or encumbrance rest with the government itself, which is legally allotted up to forty-five working days to consider each case in detail. This centralized decision-making process provides a clear pathway for authorities to assess whether potential transfers align with the country's interests, ensuring that facilities of national significance, especially those involved in digital trade infrastructure like communications or broadcast networks, remain under acceptable influence. In cases where conditions change within the transfer process, the owner must reapply, allowing the state to reassess any evolving circumstances and safeguard against unintended control shifts.

Once a decision is reached, the government communicates its stance to the applicant, and this decision holds considerable legal weight. Furthermore, a state body conducts continuous monitoring of transactions involving these strategic assets, upholding a constant layer of supervision that ensures adherence to Kazakhstan's broader objectives.

This regulatory framework directly impacts foreign investment in sectors relevant to digital trade. Many strategic facilities, such as communication lines and broadcasting systems, are integral to digital trade infrastructure. The necessity for government approval and the state's preemptive acquisition rights introduce a screening mechanism for foreign investments and mergers and acquisitions (M&A) in these sectors.

### Indicator 4.1: Patent application issues
- **Score:** 1
- **Source Legislation:** Law No. 427 on Patent, 1999 (Қазақстан Республикасының 1999 жылғы 16 шілдедегі N 427 Заңы)
- **Evidence/Impact:** According to Article 38 of Law no.427 foreign individuals, foreign legal entities and stateless persons residing in the Republic of Kazakhstan enjoy the same rights as the Kazakhstani citizens. 

However, according to Article 36(5), foreign applicants seeking to file for patent protection must operate through registered local patent attorneys. This requirement mandates that individuals or legal entities residing outside of Kazakhstan must designate a patent attorney who is a citizen of Kazakhstan and registered within the national framework to represent them in dealings with the authorized body and expert organization. This introduces a significant procedural distinction between local and foreign applicants, as local applicants are permitted to file directly without the need for such intermediaries.

Also, according to Article 35, a state fee is levied for various actions in the patent application process, including the registration of patent attorneys and the performance of examinations.

### Indicator 4.2: Patent enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies, provisional and other measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law No. 427 on Patent, 1999 (Қазақстан Республикасының 1999 жылғы 16 шілдедегі N 427 Заңы)
- **Evidence/Impact:** According to Article 22 of Law No. 427, the enforcement of intellectual property rights, particularly patents, includes both civil and administrative procedures. Once a patent application is filed, it undergoes a formal examination to ensure compliance with submission requirements, followed by a substantive examination to assess whether the invention meets legal standards of patentability, such as novelty, inventive step, and industrial applicability. If the expert agency refuses to grant a patent, the applicant has the legal right to appeal the decision before the Appeals Board. 

In Article 15, the law specifies civil enforcement mechanisms for patent infringements. Any unauthorized manufacture, sale, or commercial distribution of products that contain patented technology constitutes an infringement. Patent holders are empowered to take legal action to demand that the infringer cease their activities, compensate for damages, or surrender profits derived from the infringement.

Furthermore, Article 13 of the law incorporates provisional measures to offer legal protection during the period between patent application and formal patent issuance. This allows for interim judicial measures to prevent infringement, including the blocking of infringing goods at customs, before the final decision on the patent is made.

### Indicator 4.4: Lack of copyright framework and exceptions
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law No. 6 on Copyright and Related Rights, 1996 (Қазақстан Республикасының 1996 жылғы 10 маусымдағы N 6-I Заңы)
- **Evidence/Impact:** Kazakhstan has a copyright regime under Law of the Republic of Kazakhstan No. 6-I of June 10, 1996, on Copyright and Related Rights. Copyright exception related to fair use and fair dealing provisions can be found in Articles 19, 20 and 41 of the Copyright Law of Kazakhstan. 

The exceptions in Kazakhstan’s copyright framework, outlined in Articles 18-27 of the Law No. 427, offer exceptions for specific purposes. These exceptions include the quotation of copyrighted works, in the original language or in translation, for scientific research, criticism, or informational purposes. The reproduction, broadcasting, or communication to the public of architectural, photographic, or fine art works that are permanently located in public spaces is also permitted without the author’s consent or remuneration, provided it is not for commercial use​.

These provisions indicate that Kazakhstan follows a fair dealing approach. The exceptions are specific and confined to well-defined purposes, such as research, criticism, and private study, limiting flexibility in comparison to the broader fair use doctrine.

### Indicator 4.5: Online copyright enforcement issues: civil and administrative procedures and remedies; and provisional measures
- **Score:** 0
- **Source Legislation:** Law No. 6 on Copyright and Related Rights, 1996 (Қазақстан Республикасының 1996 жылғы 10 маусымдағы N 6-I Заңы)
- **Evidence/Impact:** According to Article 16 of Law No. 427, the reproduction, distribution, or public performance of copyrighted works, including through digital platforms, requires the copyright holder’s permission. Any unauthorized use is deemed an infringement, and sanctions can be applied.

Article 49(1) of the Law provides the mechanism for the protection of copyright through civil remedies. This includes:
- Recognition of the rights;
- Suppression of actions that infringe or threaten to infringe the rights;
- Compensation of damages, including lost profits;
- Recovery of the income received by the infringer;
- Payment of compensation in lieu of damages​

Article 49(2) outlines that the court, before considering the case, may independently issue a decision to prohibit the respondent from manufacturing, reproducing, or selling unauthorized copies of copyrighted works. It also allows for the seizure and confiscation of counterfeit goods related to copyright infringement​.

Article 48 further reinforces the protection of copyright by prohibiting unauthorized removal or alteration of rights management information and the reproduction, distribution, or public performance of works without proper authorization, particularly targeting issues related to piracy​
